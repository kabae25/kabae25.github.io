Recent Work

Agile Event-based Flight through Cluttered Environments

WPI Major Qualifying Project Video Poster

Abstract: Search and rescue applications require robots that are small and agile due to being space-constrained and highly cluttered. While Micro Aerial Vehicles (MAVs) offer the perfect size and maneuverability for these environments, autonomous navigation through cluttered settings remains a challenge; current methods struggle with high-weight payloads, requiring bulky cameras and heavy compute modules. We turn to event cameras, which offer high dynamic range, low latency, nearly no motion blur, and extreme power efficiency. In this work, we propose a modular quadrotor platform designed to be highly extensible and agile. An event-camera-based perception stack, global planner, and model predictive controller running on an onboard hardware coprocessor provide low-latency autonomy in uncertain environments. To achieve demanding motions and compensate for external forces, we utilize a lower-level adaptive attitude controller running at a high frequency.
